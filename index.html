Roman Numeral Converter

According to Britannica.com, a Roman numeral is any of the symbols employed in a numerical notation system originating from ancient Rome. These symbols include I, V, X, L, C, D, and M, which correspond to the values 1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, and 1,000, respectively, in the Hindu-Arabic numeral system.
